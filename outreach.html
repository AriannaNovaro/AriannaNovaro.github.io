I love to participate to outreach events for the popularization of science!

In 2018 I held an interactive atelier for the Science Festival in Genoa and the Researchers Night in Toulouse, with the aim of explaining voting rules to an audience of non-experts. In the same year I also gave an interactive talk about ethics and AI for high-school students at the Lycée Polyvalent Raymond Naves in Toulouse.

In 2019 I participated again to the Researchers Night in Toulouse, but this time the challenge was to bring a single object to describe what I work on. Can you guess what I brought?

In 2020 an article with title "Je décide, nous décidons... comment faire le bon choix ? Avec Arianna Novaro" (in French) was published on the University of Toulouse online magazine L'Exploreur, where in a short radio interview I explain why I am passionate to talk about research in computational social choice. That year we also collaborated with the Mairie de Toulouse to analyse the local participatory budgeting campaign, supervising a Master students project on the topic.

In 2021 an article with title "When residents design their city", about the participatory budgeting experience in Amsterdam, was published on the blog of the ILLC.
